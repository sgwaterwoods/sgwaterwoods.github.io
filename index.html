NDSS 2026 论文精选 (5/5): Web 与网络安全

NDSS 2026 系列第 5 篇: Web 与网络安全研究

本系列的最后一篇聚焦于 Web 应用安全、网络协议安全和互联网基础设施安全，涵盖 XSS 检测、Wi-Fi 安全、DNS 攻击等核心领域。

Series: Part 5 of 5 - Web & Network Security

1. Achieving Interpretable DL-based Web Attack Detection through Malicious Payload Localization

Authors: Peiyang Li (Tsinghua University & Ant Group), Fukun Mei (Tsinghua University), Ye Wang (Tsinghua University), Zhuotao Liu (Tsinghua University), Ke Xu (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Chao Shen (Xi’an Jiaotong University), Qian Wang (Wuhan University), Qi Li (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory)

English Abstract: This work achieves interpretable deep learning-based web attack detection by localizing malicious payloads, enabling security analysts to understand and verify detection decisions. The system can pinpoint exactly where in a request the attack payload is located.

中文摘要: 本研究通过定位恶意载荷实现可解释的基于深度学习的 Web 攻击检测，使安全分析师能够理解和验证检测决策。该系统可以精确定位请求中攻击载荷的位置。

2. AirSnitch: Demystifying and Breaking Client Isolation in Wi-Fi Networks

Authors: Xin’an Zhou (University of California, Riverside), Juefei Pu (University of California, Riverside), Zhutian Liu (University of California, Riverside), Zhiyun Qian (University of California, Riverside), Zhaowei Tan (University of California, Riverside), Srikanth V. Krishnamurthy (University of California, Riverside), Mathy Vanhoef (DistriNet, KU Leuven)

English Abstract: AirSnitch reveals vulnerabilities in Wi-Fi client isolation mechanisms, demonstrating practical attacks that break the security boundary between isolated clients on the same network, potentially exposing sensitive data in public Wi-Fi environments.

中文摘要: AirSnitch 揭示了 Wi-Fi 客户端隔离机制中的漏洞，展示了打破同一网络上隔离客户端之间安全边界的实际攻击，可能在公共 Wi-Fi 环境中暴露敏感数据。

3. Aliens Among Us: Observing Private or Reserved IPs on the Public Internet

Authors: Radu Anghel (TU Delft), Carlos Gañán (ICANN), Qasim Lone (RIPE NCC), Matthew Luckie (CAIDA), Yury Zhauniarovich (TU Delft)

English Abstract: This measurement study observes the prevalence of private and reserved IP addresses appearing on the public Internet, analyzing their security implications and revealing widespread misconfigurations that could enable various attacks.

中文摘要: 这项测量研究观察了私有和保留 IP 地址在公共互联网上出现的普遍性，分析其安全影响并揭示可能导致各种攻击的广泛配置错误。

4. CHAMELEOSCAN: Demystifying and Detecting iOS Chameleon Apps via LLM-Powered UI Exploration

Authors: Hongyu Lin (Zhejiang University), Yicheng Hu (Zhejiang University), Haitao Xu (Zhejiang University), Yanchen Lu (Zhejiang University), Mengxia Ren (Zhejiang University), Shuai Hao (Old Dominion University), Chuan Yue (Colorado School of Mines), Zhao Li (Hangzhou Yugu Technology), Fan Zhang (Zhejiang University), Yixin Jiang (Electric Power Research Institute)

English Abstract: CHAMELEOSCAN uses LLM-powered UI exploration to detect iOS chameleon apps that change behavior after passing App Store review, uncovering malicious apps that evade detection by behaving differently during review versus post-installation.

中文摘要: CHAMELEOSCAN 使用 LLM 驱动的 UI 探索来检测在通过 App Store 审核后改变行为的 iOS 变色龙应用，发现通过在审核期间和安装后表现不同来逃避检测的恶意应用。

5. DOM-XSS Detection via Webpage Interaction Fuzzing and URL Component Synthesis

Authors: Nuno Sabino (Carnegie Mellon University, Instituto Superior Técnico, Universidade de Lisboa, and Instituto de Telecomunicações), Darion Cassel (Carnegie Mellon University), Rui Abreu (Universidade do Porto, INESC-ID), Pedro Adão (Instituto Superior Técnico, Universidade de Lisboa, and Instituto de Telecomunicações), Lujo Bauer (Carnegie Mellon University), Limin Jia (Carnegie Mellon University)

English Abstract: This work proposes a novel approach to detect DOM-based XSS vulnerabilities through automated webpage interaction fuzzing and intelligent URL component synthesis, significantly improving detection coverage over existing tools.

中文摘要: 本研究提出了一种通过自动化网页交互模糊测试和智能 URL 组件合成来检测基于 DOM 的 XSS 漏洞的新方法，显著提高了现有工具的检测覆盖率。

6. CoordMail: Exploiting SMTP Timeout and Command Interaction to Coordinate Email Middleware for Convergence Amplification Attack

Authors: Ruixuan Li (Tsinghua University), Chaoyi Lu (Zhongguancun Laboratory), Baojun Liu (Tsinghua University), Yanzhong Lin (Coremail Technology Co. Ltd), Qingfeng Pan (Coremail Technology Co. Ltd), Jun Shao (Zhejiang Gongshang University; Zhejiang Key Laboratory of Big Data and Future E-Commerce Technology)

English Abstract: CoordMail exploits SMTP timeout and command interaction mechanisms to coordinate email middleware for convergence amplification attacks, demonstrating a novel attack vector that can amplify malicious traffic significantly.

中文摘要: CoordMail 利用 SMTP 超时和命令交互机制来协调电子邮件中间件进行收敛放大攻击，展示了一种可以显著放大恶意流量的新型攻击向量。

7. Continuous User Behavior Monitoring using DNS Cache Timing Attacks

Authors: Hannes Weissteiner (Graz University of Technology), Roland Czerny (Graz University of Technology), Simone Franza (Graz University of Technology), Stefan Gast (Graz University of Technology), Johanna Ullrich (University of Vienna), Daniel Gruss (Graz University of Technology)

English Abstract: This research demonstrates how DNS cache timing attacks can be used for continuous user behavior monitoring, revealing visited websites and online activities without requiring network access or malware installation.

中文摘要: 本研究展示了如何使用 DNS 缓存时序攻击进行持续的用户行为监控，在不需要网络访问或恶意软件安装的情况下揭示访问的网站和在线活动。

8. Crack in the Armor: Underlying Infrastructure Threats to RPKI Publication Point Reachability

Authors: Yunhao Liu (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Jessie Hui Wang (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Yuedong Xu (Fudan University), Zongpeng Li (Tsinghua University), Yangyang Wang (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Jilong Wang (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory)

English Abstract: This work identifies underlying infrastructure threats to RPKI (Resource Public Key Infrastructure) publication point reachability, revealing vulnerabilities that could undermine the security of internet routing.

中文摘要: 本研究识别了对 RPKI (资源公钥基础设施) 发布点可达性的底层基础设施威胁，揭示了可能破坏互联网路由安全的漏洞。

9. Demystifying RPKI-Invalid Prefixes: Hidden Causes and Security Risks

Authors: Weitong Li (Virginia Tech), Tao Wan (CableLabs), Tijay Chung (Virginia Tech)

English Abstract: This study demystifies RPKI-invalid prefixes by uncovering hidden causes and analyzing their security risks, providing insights into why valid network operators may inadvertently create invalid RPKI records.

中文摘要: 本研究通过揭示隐藏原因和分析其安全风险来揭开 RPKI 无效前缀的神秘面纱，提供了为什么合法网络运营商可能无意中创建无效 RPKI 记录的见解。

10. Enhancing Website Fingerprinting Attacks against Traffic Drift

Authors: Xinhao Deng (Tsinghua University & Ant Group), Yixiang Zhang (Tsinghua University), Qi Li (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Zhuotao Liu (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory), Yabo Wang (Tsinghua University), Ke Xu (Tsinghua University & Zhongguancun Laboratory)

English Abstract: This research enhances website fingerprinting attacks to remain effective against traffic drift, demonstrating that even when websites’ traffic patterns change over time, sophisticated fingerprinting attacks can still compromise user privacy.

中文摘要: 本研究增强网站指纹攻击以对抗流量漂移保持有效，证明即使网站的流量模式随时间变化，复杂的指纹攻击仍然可以损害用户隐私。

研究趋势 / Research Trends

Web 与网络安全研究的主要趋势：

  1. 可解释性需求: 从黑盒检测转向可解释的安全分析，帮助分析师理解威胁
  2. 基础设施安全: 关注 DNS、RPKI、SMTP 等互联网基础协议的安全问题
  3. 隐私威胁进化: 时序攻击、流量分析等新型隐私威胁持续演进
  4. 移动应用安全: iOS、Android 应用的恶意行为检测和审核绕过
  5. 自动化检测: LLM 驱动的自动化漏洞发现和应用分析

实际影响:

  • 企业 Web 安全: 可解释的攻击检测帮助安全团队快速响应
  • 公共 Wi-Fi 安全: 客户端隔离漏洞影响咖啡馆、酒店等公共场所
  • 互联网路由安全: RPKI 漏洞可能导致大规模路由劫持
  • 用户隐私: DNS 缓存时序攻击和网站指纹威胁在线隐私
  • 应用商店安全: 变色龙应用检测保护移动用户

NDSS 2026 论文精选 (4/5): 密码学与隐私

NDSS 2026 系列第 4 篇: 密码学与隐私保护研究

本文聚焦于密码学协议、隐私保护技术和安全多方计算，涵盖私有信息检索、加密消息系统、隐私保护机器学习等前沿领域。

Series: Part 4 of 5 - Cryptography & Privacy

1. Abuse Resistant Traceability with Minimal Trust for Encrypted Messaging Systems

Authors: Zhongming Wang (Chongqing University), Tao Xiang (Chongqing University), Xiaoguo Li (Chongqing University), Guomin Yang (Singapore Management University), Biwen Chen (Chongqing University), Ze Jiang (Chongqing University), Jiacheng Wang (Nanyang Technological University), Chuan Ma (Chongqing University), Robert H. Deng (Singapore Management University)

English Abstract: This work proposes an abuse-resistant traceability mechanism for encrypted messaging systems that minimizes trust requirements while enabling accountability. The system prevents malicious actors from abusing the traceability feature while still allowing legitimate law enforcement access.

中文摘要: 本研究为加密消息系统提出了一种抗滥用的可追溯机制，在实现问责的同时最小化信任需求。该系统防止恶意行为者滥用可追溯功能，同时仍允许合法的执法访问。

2. BKPIR: Keyword PIR for Private Boolean Retrieval

Authors: Jie Song (Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences; Intelligent Policing Key Laboratory of Sichuan Province, Sichuan Police College; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences), Zhen Xu (Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences), Yan Zhang (Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences), Pengwei Zhan (Sangfor Technologies Inc.), Mingxuan Li (School of Criminal Investigation, People’s Public Security University of China), Shuai Ma (SKLCCSE Lab, Beihang University), Ru Xie (Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences)

English Abstract: BKPIR introduces an efficient keyword-based private information retrieval scheme specifically designed for private Boolean retrieval operations, enabling users to query databases without revealing what they’re searching for.

中文摘要: BKPIR 引入了一种专为私有布尔检索操作设计的高效基于关键字的私有信息检索方案，使用户能够在不透露搜索内容的情况下查询数据库。

3. Cryptobazaar: Private Sealed-bid Auctions at Scale

Authors: Andrija Novakovic (Bain Capital Crypto), Alireza Kavousi (University College London), Kobi Gurkan (Bain Capital Crypto), Philipp Jovanovic (University College London)

English Abstract: Cryptobazaar enables private sealed-bid auctions at scale using cryptographic techniques, ensuring bid privacy while maintaining auction integrity. The system supports thousands of participants without compromising performance or security.

中文摘要: Cryptobazaar 使用密码学技术实现大规模私有密封投标拍卖，确保投标隐私同时保持拍卖完整性。该系统支持数千名参与者，而不会影响性能或安全性。

4. CryptPEFT: Efficient and Private Neural Network Inference via Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning

Authors: Saisai Xia (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS), Wenhao Wang (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS), Zihao Wang (Nanyang Technological University (NTU)), Yuhui Zhang (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS), Yier Jin (University of Science and Technology of China), Dan Meng (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS), Rui Hou (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS)

English Abstract: CryptPEFT achieves efficient and private neural network inference by combining cryptographic techniques with parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, enabling secure ML inference in cloud environments.

中文摘要: CryptPEFT 通过将密码学技术与参数高效微调方法相结合，实现高效且私密的神经网络推理，使云环境中的安全 ML 推理成为可能。

5. cwPSU: Efficient Unbalanced Private Set Union via Constant-weight Codes

Authors: Qingwen Li (Xidian University), Song Bian (Beihang University), Hui Li (Xidian University)

English Abstract: cwPSU proposes an efficient solution for unbalanced private set union using constant-weight codes, significantly improving performance over existing approaches when set sizes differ dramatically.

中文摘要: cwPSU 提出了一种使用常权码进行不平衡私有集合并集的高效解决方案，当集合大小差异很大时，显著提高了现有方法的性能。

6. Cirrus: Performant and Accountable Distributed SNARK

Authors: Wenhao Wang (Yale University, IC3), Fangyan Shi (Tsinghua University), Dani Vilardell (Cornell Tech, IC3), Fan Zhang (Yale University, IC3)

English Abstract: Cirrus introduces a distributed SNARK (Succinct Non-interactive Argument of Knowledge) system that is both performant and accountable, enabling scalable zero-knowledge proof generation across multiple parties.

中文摘要: Cirrus 引入了一个高性能且可问责的分布式 SNARK (简洁非交互式知识论证) 系统，实现跨多方的可扩展零知识证明生成。

7. CTng: Secure Certificate and Revocation Transparency

Authors: Jie Kong (Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT), Damon James (Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT), Hemi Leibowitz (Faculty of Computer Science, The College of Management Academic Studies, Rishon LeZion, Israel), Ewa Syta (Dept. of Computer Science, Trinity College, Hartford, CT), Amir Herzberg (Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT)

English Abstract: CTng proposes a secure certificate and revocation transparency system that improves upon existing certificate transparency logs by providing stronger security guarantees against malicious certificate authorities.

中文摘要: CTng 提出了一个安全的证书和撤销透明度系统，通过提供针对恶意证书颁发机构的更强安全保证来改进现有的证书透明度日志。

8. Convergent Privacy Framework for Multi-layer GNNs through Contractive Message Passing

Authors: Yu Zheng (University of California, Irvine), Chenang Li (University of California, Irvine), Zhou Li (University of California, Irvine), Qingsong Wang (University of California, San Diego)

English Abstract: This work presents a convergent privacy framework for multi-layer graph neural networks (GNNs) through contractive message passing, providing formal privacy guarantees for graph-based machine learning.

中文摘要: 本研究通过收缩消息传递为多层图神经网络 (GNN) 提出了一个收敛隐私框架，为基于图的机器学习提供正式的隐私保证。

9. Distributed Broadcast Encryption for Confidential Interoperability across Private Blockchains

Authors: Angelo De Caro (IBM Research Zurich), Kaoutar Elkhiyaoui (IBM Research Zurich), Sandeep Nishad (IBM Research India), Sikhar Patranabis (IBM Research India), Venkatraman Ramakrishna (IBM Research India)

English Abstract: This research enables confidential interoperability across private blockchains through distributed broadcast encryption, allowing secure data sharing between different blockchain networks without compromising privacy.

中文摘要: 本研究通过分布式广播加密实现跨私有区块链的机密互操作性，允许在不同区块链网络之间安全共享数据而不损害隐私。

10. Faster Than Ever: A New Lightweight Private Set Intersection and Its Variants

Authors: Guowei Ling (Shanghai Jiaotong University), Peng Tang (Shanghai Jiao Tong University), Jinyong Shan (Beijing Smartchip Microelectronics Technology Co., Ltd.), Liyao Xiang (Shanghai Jiao Tong University), Weidong Qiu (School of Cyber Science and Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China)

English Abstract: This work presents a new lightweight private set intersection (PSI) protocol that achieves record-breaking performance, enabling practical privacy-preserving data analysis at scale.

中文摘要: 本研究提出了一种新的轻量级私有集合交集 (PSI) 协议，实现了破纪录的性能，使大规模隐私保护数据分析成为实用。

研究趋势 / Research Trends

密码学与隐私保护研究的主要趋势：

  1. 实用性优先: 从理论证明转向可部署的实际系统，性能优化成为关键
  2. 隐私保护 ML: 将密码学技术与机器学习结合，实现私密推理和训练
  3. 零知识证明应用: SNARK、分布式 ZKP 等技术在区块链和隐私计算中广泛应用
  4. 私有信息检索进化: 从基础 PIR 到关键字 PIR、布尔检索等更复杂场景
  5. 区块链隐私: 跨链互操作、隐私保护智能合约等区块链特定的密码学问题

实际应用价值:

  • 金融科技: 私有拍卖、隐私保护交易
  • 医疗健康: 隐私保护的医疗数据分析
  • 云计算: 加密数据上的机器学习推理
  • 区块链: 隐私币、隐私保护智能合约
  • 通信安全: 端到端加密消息系统

NDSS 2026 论文精选 (3/5): IoT 与嵌入式系统安全

NDSS 2026 系列第 3 篇: IoT 与嵌入式系统安全研究

本文聚焦于物联网设备、嵌入式系统和工业控制系统的安全问题，涵盖固件分析、机器人系统、PLC 安全等关键领域。

Series: Part 3 of 5 - IoT & Embedded Security

1. ADGFUZZ: Assignment Dependency-Guided Fuzzing for Robotic Vehicles

Authors: Yuncheng Wang (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China), Yaowen Zheng (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China), Puzhuo Liu (Ant Group; Tsinghua University), Dongliang Fang (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China), Jiaxing Cheng (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China), Dingyi Shi (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China), Limin Sun (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS, Beijing, China; School of Cyber Security, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China)

English Abstract: ADGFUZZ introduces assignment dependency-guided fuzzing specifically designed for robotic vehicles, significantly improving bug discovery in autonomous systems by analyzing variable dependencies in control logic.

中文摘要: ADGFUZZ 引入了专为机器人车辆设计的赋值依赖引导模糊测试，通过分析控制逻辑中的变量依赖关系显著提高了自动系统中的错误发现率。

2. An LLM-Driven Fuzzing Framework for Detecting Logic Instruction Bugs in PLCs

Authors: Jiaxing Cheng (Institute of Information Engineering, CAS; School of Cyber Security, UCAS), Ming Zhou (School of Cyber Science and Engineering, Nanjing University of Science and Technology), Haining Wang (Virginia Tech), Xin Chen (Institute of Institute of Information Engineering, CAS; School of Cyber Security, UCAS), Yuncheng Wang (Institute of Institute of Information Engineering, CAS; School of Cyber Security, UCAS), Yibo Qu (Institute of Institute of Information Engineering, CAS; School of Cyber Security, UCAS), Limin Sun (Institute of Institute of Information Engineering, CAS; School of Cyber Security, UCAS)

English Abstract: This work leverages LLMs to drive fuzzing for programmable logic controllers (PLCs), detecting critical logic instruction bugs in industrial control systems that traditional fuzzing methods miss.

中文摘要: 本研究利用 LLM 驱动可编程逻辑控制器 (PLC) 的模糊测试，检测传统模糊测试方法遗漏的工业控制系统中的关键逻辑指令错误。

3. FirmAgent: Leveraging Fuzzing to Assist LLM Agents with IoT Firmware Vulnerability Discovery

Authors: Jiangan Ji (Information Engineering University,Tsinghua University), Chao Zhang (Tsinghua University), Shuitao Gan (Labortory for Advanced Computing and Intelligence Engineering), Lin Jian (Information Engineering University), Hangtian Liu (Information Engineering University), Tieming Liu (Information Engineering University), Lei Zheng (Tsinghua university), Zhipeng Jia (Information Engineering University)

English Abstract: FirmAgent combines fuzzing with LLM agents to enhance IoT firmware vulnerability discovery, bridging the gap between automated testing and intelligent analysis to uncover complex vulnerabilities.

中文摘要: FirmAgent 将模糊测试与 LLM 代理相结合，增强物联网固件漏洞发现，弥合自动化测试和智能分析之间的差距以发现复杂漏洞。

4. FirmCross: Detecting Taint-style Vulnerabilities in Modern C-Lua Hybrid Web Services of Linux-based Firmware

Authors: Runhao Liu (National University of Defense Technology), Jiarun Dai (Fudan University), Haoyu Xiao (Fudan University), Yuan Zhang (Fudan University), Yeqi Mou (National University of Defense Technology), Lukai Xu (National University of Defense Technology), Bo Yu (National University of Defense Technology), Baosheng Wang (National University of Defense Technology), Min Yang (Fudan University)

English Abstract: FirmCross addresses the challenge of detecting taint-style vulnerabilities in modern firmware that uses hybrid C-Lua web services, common in router and IoT device firmware where untrusted input can flow across language boundaries.

中文摘要: FirmCross 解决了在使用混合 C-Lua Web 服务的现代固件中检测污点式漏洞的挑战，这在路由器和物联网设备固件中很常见，不受信任的输入可以跨语言边界流动。

5. FlyTrap: Physical Distance-Pulling Attack Towards Camera-based Autonomous Target Tracking Systems

Authors: [Full author list from NDSS website]

English Abstract: FlyTrap demonstrates physical distance-pulling attacks against camera-based autonomous tracking systems, manipulating perceived distances through carefully crafted physical adversarial patterns to compromise safety-critical applications like autonomous vehicles and drones.

Latest Cybersecurity Research Highlights - March 2026

This week brings exciting developments in cybersecurity research from arXiv, covering everything from automated scam detection to IoT security innovations and AI safety. Here’s a comprehensive summary of the most impactful papers published in late February and early March 2026.

1. Anansi: Scalable Job Scam Detection System

Paper: “Scalable Characterization of Message-Based Job Scams”
Published: February 27, 2026
arXiv: 2602.24223

Key Findings

Researchers have developed Anansi, the first scalable end-to-end measurement pipeline designed to systematically engage with and analyze job-based smishing scams. This automated system combines:

  • Large Language Models (LLMs) for natural conversation with scammers
  • Automated browser agents to interact with scam infrastructure
  • Infrastructure fingerprinting tools for tracking campaigns

Impact

The system successfully:

  • Collected over 29,000 scam messages
  • Interacted with more than 1,900 scammers
  • Identified millions of dollars in cryptocurrency losses
  • Uncovered extensive reuse of message templates, domains, and crypto wallets

Significance

This research demonstrates the feasibility of automating scammer engagement and infrastructure analysis, offering a new methodological foundation for studying large-scale fraud ecosystems. The system revealed sophisticated deceptive techniques including domain fronting and brand impersonation.

2. SAILOR: Ultra-Lightweight RISC-V for IoT Security

Paper: “A Scalable and Energy-Efficient Ultra-Lightweight RISC-V for IoT Security”
Published: February 27, 2026
arXiv: 2602.24166

Innovation

SAILOR introduces a revolutionary modular RISC-V core family specifically designed for cryptographic applications in IoT devices. The design spans multiple data-path widths (1-, 2-, 4-, 8-, 16-, and 32-bit) to optimize for different use cases.

Performance Achievements

  • 13x improvement in performance and energy efficiency over state-of-the-art solutions
  • 59% reduction in area (hardware footprint)
  • Minimal overhead for integrating RISC-V cryptography extensions

Why It Matters

Most existing RISC-V cores for IoT prioritize either area or energy efficiency, often compromising security. SAILOR demonstrates that lightweight cryptographic features can be integrated without prohibitive overhead, making secure IoT devices more practical and affordable.

3. Jailbreak Foundry: Reproducible LLM Security Testing

Paper: “Jailbreak Foundry: From Papers to Runnable Attacks for Reproducible Benchmarking”
Published: February 27, 2026
arXiv: 2602.24009

The Problem

Jailbreak techniques for large language models evolve faster than benchmarks, making robustness estimates quickly outdated. Different papers use different datasets and judging protocols, making comparisons difficult.

The Solution

JAILBREAK FOUNDRY (JBF) uses a multi-agent workflow to automatically translate jailbreak research papers into executable attack modules. The system features:

  • JBF-LIB: Shared contracts and reusable utilities
  • JBF-FORGE: Multi-agent paper-to-module translation
  • JBF-EVAL: Standardized evaluation framework

Results

Across 30 reproduced attacks, JBF achieved:

  • High fidelity with mean deviation of only +0.26 percentage points from reported results
  • 82.5% mean reused-code ratio, reducing implementation effort
  • 50% reduction in attack-specific code compared to original repositories

This system enables standardized evaluation of attacks across 10 victim models, creating “living benchmarks” that keep pace with the rapidly shifting security landscape.

4. Feature Transferability in IoT Botnet Detection

Paper: “Exploring Robust Intrusion Detection: A Benchmark Study of Feature Transferability in IoT Botnet Attack Detection”
Published: February 27, 2026
arXiv: 2602.23874
Conference: Accepted for ICISSP 2026

Research Focus

This comprehensive study evaluates how well intrusion detection features transfer across different IoT network environments—a critical challenge as network characteristics vary significantly across domains.

Methodology

The research evaluated three widely-used flow-based feature sets:

  • Argus
  • Zeek
  • CICFlowMeter

These were tested across four heterogeneous datasets representing various IoT and Industrial IoT conditions.

Key Findings

  • Models trained on one domain suffer significant performance degradation when applied to different target domains
  • Feature choice and classification algorithm critically impact transferability
  • Using SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP), researchers identified which features remain robust across domains

Practical Impact

The paper provides actionable guidelines for:

  • Feature engineering to improve robustness under domain variability
  • Algorithm selection for cross-domain resilience
  • Designing effective intrusion detection that works across diverse IoT environments

Emerging Trends

These papers collectively highlight several important trends in cybersecurity research:

  1. Automation is Key: From Anansi’s automated scam detection to JBF’s automated attack reproduction, automation is becoming central to keeping pace with evolving threats.

  2. Efficiency Meets Security: SAILOR demonstrates that security and efficiency are not mutually exclusive—critical for resource-constrained IoT devices.

  3. Cross-Domain Robustness: The IoT botnet study emphasizes that security solutions must work across diverse environments, not just in controlled lab settings.

  4. AI Safety Ecosystem: JBF’s living benchmark approach shows the community is building infrastructure for continuous security evaluation as LLMs evolve.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity landscape in March 2026 reflects a maturing field where researchers are not just finding vulnerabilities but building scalable systems to detect, prevent, and evaluate security threats across diverse domains—from cryptocurrency scams to IoT botnets to AI safety.

These advances represent significant steps toward more automated, efficient, and robust security solutions that can adapt to rapidly evolving threat landscapes.

IH&MMSec 由原先的 IH 和 ACM MM&Sec 两个 Workshops 合并而来。

For over a decade, two workshops have been shaping the landscape of research in multimedia security. On one hand, Information Hiding (aka. IH) was created in 1996 and focused on digital watermarking, steganography and steganalysis, anonymity and privacy, hard to intercept communications and covert/subliminal channels. On the other hand, the ACM Workshop on Multimedia and Security (aka. ACM MM&Sec) was initiated in 1998 and focused on data hiding, robust/perceptual hash, biometrics, video surveillance, and multimedia forensics. Key seminal works have been published in these two workshops and papers accepted for publication there attracted over 9,000 citations in total. Year after year, the two communities grew closer and the overlap between their respective scope got bigger. As a result, after 14 successful editions each, IH and ACM MMSec decided to merge in a single event in an attempt to establish synergies between the two communities while building on the reputation obtained over the years. [Link]
